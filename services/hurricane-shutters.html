Hurricane Shutters in Broward County

Cost-effective storm protection for South Florida homes and businesses. Accordion, roll-down, and storm panel options available.

Deployable Storm Protection

Hurricane Shutters for Every Budget

Hurricane shutters provide code-compliant storm protection at a lower upfront cost than full impact window replacement. They're an excellent choice for budget-conscious homeowners, rental properties, and as supplemental protection for homes with existing windows.

At Safehouse Shutter, we install all major shutter types throughout Plantation, Fort Lauderdale, and Broward County. Every system meets Florida Building Code and carries Miami-Dade NOA certification. We'll help you choose the right solution based on your budget, property type, and ease-of-use requirements.

For homes that already have impact windows, shutters can provide additional protection for large openings or areas where permanent solutions aren't practical. Many homeowners combine both for comprehensive coverage.

Aluminum Storm Panels

Removable ‚Ä¢ Budget-Friendly ‚Ä¢ Proven Protection

Storm panels are corrugated aluminum sheets that attach to pre-installed tracks around your windows. They offer excellent protection at the lowest cost per opening. Panels must be stored when not in use and require 1-2 hours to deploy for a typical home.

Deployment: 1-2 hours
Storage: Garage/shed required
Cost: Most affordable

Best for: Budget-conscious homeowners, secondary homes, DIY-minded owners, rental properties.

Why Shutters

Benefits of Hurricane Shutters

Budget-Friendly Protection

Hurricane shutters cost significantly less than impact windows while providing the same code-compliant storm protection. They're ideal for homeowners who need protection now without a major investment.

Code-Compliant

All our shutters meet Florida Building Code and carry Miami-Dade NOA certification. They satisfy Broward County's High-Velocity Hurricane Zone requirements for opening protection.

Security When Away

Shutters can be deployed when you're traveling to protect against break-ins. Roll-down and accordion shutters are particularly effective deterrents for vacant homes.

Insurance Discounts

Hurricane shutters can qualify you for wind mitigation discounts on your homeowner's insurance. We provide all documentation needed for your insurance company.

Hurricane Shutters FAQ

Hurricane shutter costs vary by type:

  • Storm panels: $7-15 per square foot installed
  • Accordion shutters: $15-25 per square foot installed
  • Roll-down shutters: $25-45 per square foot installed

A typical Broward County home might spend $2,000-5,000 for storm panels, $5,000-12,000 for accordion shutters, or $10,000-25,000 for roll-down systems. We provide free estimates with exact pricing for your property.

Yes, hurricane shutters provide equivalent storm protection to impact windows when properly deployed. Both meet Florida Building Code requirements for the High-Velocity Hurricane Zone.

The key difference is that shutters must be deployed before each storm, while impact windows protect 24/7 without any action. Impact windows also provide daily benefits like energy efficiency and noise reduction that shutters don't offer.

Most residential shutter installations take 1-2 days depending on the number of openings and shutter type. Accordion and roll-down shutters take slightly longer than storm panel track installation.

Lead time from order to installation is typically 2-4 weeks, shorter than impact windows which require 4-6 weeks for manufacturing.

If you have impact-rated windows and doors on all openings, you typically don't need shutters for code compliance. However, some homeowners add shutters for:

  • Extra protection layer during major hurricanes
  • Security when away for extended periods
  • Large openings where impact products would be very expensive
