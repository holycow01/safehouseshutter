Why Impact Doors Matter in South Florida

Your doors are the largest openings in your home ‚Äî and the most vulnerable during a hurricane. Impact doors eliminate this risk with hurricane-rated glass and reinforced frames designed for South Florida storms.

In South Florida's High-Velocity Hurricane Zone (HVHZ) ‚Äî which includes all of Broward County, Miami-Dade, and Palm Beach counties ‚Äî Florida Building Code requires hurricane protection on all exterior openings for new construction and major renovations.

You can meet this requirement with either:

  • Impact-rated doors and windows (permanent protection)
  • Approved hurricane shutters (must be deployed before storms)

Impact doors meet code requirements without needing separate shutters, providing 24/7 protection and daily benefits like security and energy efficiency.

Both meet Florida hurricane code requirements, but they work differently:

Impact Doors:

  • Hurricane-rated glass and reinforced frame built-in
  • 24/7 protection ‚Äî no preparation before storms
  • Daily benefits: security, energy efficiency, noise reduction
  • Higher upfront cost, but no maintenance or storage of shutters

Regular Doors with Shutters:

  • Must deploy shutters before each storm
  • Protection only when shutters are in place
  • Lower upfront cost
  • Requires shutter storage and maintenance
